Dear AzerothCore community,
2020 has not been a good year for the world out there, but it definitely has been a great year for our project.
We are the biggest open-source WoW emulation community of 2020. During the year, we pushed improvements to the master branch every day. New contributors joined the project, and several old ones came back. We fixed plenty of game content and added new features. We introduced unit-testing. We made our application more stable by fixing some critical issues. We kept on updating our codebase by replacing legacy code with a modern and clean C++. We completed Keira3 (started in mid-2019) by providing the best WoW world-Database editor available in the market and tailored this application specifically for AzerothCore.
We improved our CI by adding many checks, which will prevent new PRs from breaking the build or adding new code warnings on all major platforms and compilers. I firmly believe that this, along with our dry-run and DB error check, makes our CI the best you can find in WoW emulation. We improved the pathfinding mechanism. We added tons of translation strings to improve the gaming experience for non-English clients. We added plenty of modules and custom tools too because variety is the spice of life! We did many other improvements that are just too many to be mentioned here. Finally, we launched the child-project ChromieCraft - we basically aim to fix the game content from A to Z, and we've got very satisfying results so far. When I say "we", of course, I mean the entire AzerothCore community, as none of the above-mentioned outcomes would have been achieved without such a strong, friendly, and collaborative community.

Solid base

AzerothCore has a strong and solid base, coming from years of development: it is based on MaNGOS, TrinityCore and SunwellCore.
We're improving this reliability offering a project that follow specific versioning rules, allowing you to develop in the right way.

Playable

Our main goal is to provide a fully working and playable server application. We will focus our stable revisions on final user experiences, instead of following strict development patterns like our predecessors.
Of course we will work to improve the code in every new versions that are under development stage.

Open Source

AzerothCore modules are released under GNU AGPL 3.0 and GNU GPL 2.0, both those liceses are totally free and open source. We believe in the free software, we love cooperation.

Tested, realiable, updated

SunwellCore, the father of AzerothCore, had been running with thousands of players for years. The goal of AzerothCore is to keep this game application updated, continuing its development.

Community-driven

The main strenght of AzerothCore is its community. Users can help us by improving our code base or simply by submitting any bug report. If you want to contribute, we'd love it!
